Frequently
Asked
Questions

What is Founderpath and how does it work?

Connect your billing tools — Stripe, Chargebee, or QuickBooks — and get an instant offer based on your MRR. Accept, sign, and capital lands in your account in 24–72 hours. You repay as a flat percentage of monthly revenue, so payments flex naturally with your business.

Who qualifies to get funding through Founderpath?

Founderpath works with founders running B2B SaaS companies with at least $500k in last year revenue, healthy retention, and recurring subscription contracts. Founderpath also funds ecommerce brands with at least $500k of last year sales and agencies who do more than $1m per year in revenue.

How is Founderpath different from venture capital or traditional bank loans?

No equity, no board seats, no personal guarantees. Unlike VC you keep full ownership. Unlike bank loans, approval is based on your live revenue data — not credit history or collateral. You can have a real offer in minutes, not months.

What type of funding products does Founderpath offer?

Founderpath offers revenue-based financing, recurring revenue loans, and revolving credit lines. The right product depends on your MRR, growth rate, and how you plan to deploy the capital.

How fast can I get capital after applying?

Most founders receive an offer within minutes of connecting their tools. Once you accept and sign, funds land in your bank account within 24–72 hours.

Do I need to give up equity or board seats to use Founderpath?

Never. Founderpath is 100% non-dilutive. You keep full ownership of your company and no board seats or observers are ever required.

How does Founderpath evaluate my company for financing?

We analyze your MRR, churn rate, growth trajectory, and customer retention using live data directly from your billing tools. No manual underwriting, no pitch decks — just your actual revenue performance.

What sizes of loans or credit lines are available?

Offers range from $10,000 to $10,000,000. The amount is calculated as a multiple of your MRR, so it scales directly with the size of your business.

Are there personal guarantees, liens, or warrants required?

No personal guarantees, no liens on personal assets, and no warrants. Your company's recurring revenue is the only security needed.

What happens if my company's growth slows after I take capital?

Repayments are tied to a percentage of your monthly revenue. If revenue dips, your payment dips proportionally. There are no fixed monthly minimums that could strain your runway during a slow period.

Is my financial data safe when I connect Stripe, QuickBooks, or HubSpot?

Yes. We use read-only API connections and bank-grade encryption. Your data is never sold or shared with third parties.

Can I repay early without penalty?

Yes. Early repayment is always an option at no extra cost. Many founders pay down their balance when they have a strong revenue month and redraw when they need capital again.

Who are some SaaS founders that have used Founderpath, and what results have they seen?

Founders at BadgerMaps, Mangomint, Reply.io, Kissflow, MoEngage, and hundreds more have used Founderpath to fund hiring, marketing, and product expansion without giving up equity. Many have returned for multiple rounds of follow-on capital.

Does Founderpath do deals outside the US?

Founderpath currently focuses on US-incorporated companies. International founders with a US entity are welcome to apply — reach out to discuss your specific situation.

Is Founderpath SOC 2 and GDPR compliant?

Yes. Founderpath is SOC 2 Type II certified and GDPR compliant. Data security and privacy are foundational to how we operate.
